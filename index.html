"What is Poetic about Computation?"

By Taeyoon Choi

  1. Subject: This chapter discusses how code is just one aspect of computation and how it holds aesthetic value and is considered a form of poetry.

  2. Context: Taeyoon Choi wrote this in 2017. The book is based off some of his lectures at the School for Poetic Computation, where he is the co-founder. The reading goes in depth about the origin of the first computer and different types of computers.
    • First computers were humans
    • Mechanical computer: could calculate large sets of data and was a system of moving gears
    • Analog computer: used a continuous electrical signal and used a disc and wheel mechanism
    • Electronic computer: used electrical currents to carry out more complex logic
    Choi was intrigued by the fact that computers essentially only use 0s and 1s to carry out all functions. He then made a computer from scratch and showed how it really is an art form.

  3. Observation: I find it kind of crazy how new computers are, considering the first computer was invented in the mid 1900s, and how quickly they have advanced to what they are today.

  4. Question: The reading briefly touched on the topic of computing and ethics. What is considered ethical and unethical when it comes to computing?

  5. Lesson: I had never though about coding in this sense and had never considered the actual syntax and written aspect a form of art.

  6. Connection: This reading reminded me about the first project we did in which we made different typographic layouts for a poem. While the text was obviously poetic, so was the code. Most of us used a lot of spaces " " and line breaks, and the repition of these in the code could be interpreted as poetic.
